German WWII indoor training cartridge fired through a sub-caliber insert installed directly inside a standard service pistol. Allowed soldiers to practice marksmanship indoors using their actual duty weapon without live ammunition logistics.

The Wehrmacht faced a training ammunition shortage as WWII progressed — live 9mm Parabellum was too valuable to burn on stationary target practice, and dedicated training ranges were being destroyed by Allied bombing. It represents one of the most ingenious training ammunition solutions ever devised: zero retraining on a different weapon, zero cost of a separate training pistol, and usable in any bombed-out basement.
